CULTURICO

Get ready for your next journey to explore the world of wonders!

This is a UX/UI case study of a native iOS and Android mobile app.

CULTURICO is a visual-first travel reservation app for anyone who likes to travel and experience local cultures. It is an app for people who want to explore new places, gain new experience, and to meet new people.

Why CULTURICO?

Through this app, hosts have the flexibility to be creative and find ways to promote, attract and get people interested in their trips The app also allows customers to have a better understanding of what these trips have to offer.

Skills employed

Incorporate design systems (iOS and Android) to create an app that fits the requirements of two different systems. Also conducted user interviews with both iOS users and Android users to get real-life opinions on the design and layout of these native apps.

Tools: Figma

Problem

Majority of the apps currently available are relatively text-based, where visual content are usually treated as supplementary elements. While words do provide detailed information, visual content is the key to attract and persuade people to make a purchase. In this case, book a trip.

User Testing and Feedback

After creating the high fidelity wireframes, I've reached out to 3 iPhone users and 2 Android phone users to ask for responses and feedback. Here's a summary from these 5 users:

iOS User

What they like about:

  1. Simple and clean
  2. Easy to navigate
  3. Familiar app navigations
  4. Provides an option for users to sign up for an account whenever they want
  5. Visual-first approach

What changes they would like to see:

  • A direct access to 'wishlist' (trips that they have saved)
  • A consistant color choice throughout the app (odd yellow bar in one of the screens)
  • A separate 'Review' section under the 'Trip' page

Android User

What they like about:

  • Large and visually attracting visuals content
  • Simple and clean design
  • Easy to read

What changes they would like to see:

  • Not necessarily to have different sets of designs. Might be useful to have similar design so users can access the app even when switching between dfferent systems.
  • Majority of the design can be based on iOS design
  • Lacking a section to access all the trips a user haave saved
  • 'My Profile' page design seems outdated - content in boxes (with borders) not visually pleasing

Design Adjustments

Upon receiving these feedbacks, I've went back to my project and made some modifications incorporating their suggestions and applying them to my design.

Challenges & Takeaways

The biggest challenge for me is to figure out the Android system and design. As a full iOS user without any Android devices with me, I had to rely on official guideline, screenshots share online, other designers' work, and visit phone stores to have a basic understanding of how Android phones work and look. The key takeaway here is that it is always highly recommended to reach out to people for user interview/testing when designing apps. After all, the role and purpose of UX/UI Designers is to create products that people will actually use. This is particular useful since those people are actual Android phone users, their answers and feedbacks will not only reflect how Android phone works, but also to provide actual user experiences.
